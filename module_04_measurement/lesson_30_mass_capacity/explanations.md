## Understanding Mass

### What Does Mass Mean?

Think of mass as **"how much stuff"** is in something. A heavy object has more mass, a light object has less mass.

**Examples:**
- A basketball has less mass than a bowling ball (basketball is lighter)
- A pencil has less mass than a dictionary (pencil is lighter)
- Your body has more mass than a piece of paper (you're heavier)

### Grams (g)

**What is one gram?**
Imagine a sugar cube. It weighs about 1 gram.

**Common items and their masses:**
```
Paper clip: 1 g
Marble: 6-7 g
Battery (AA): 23 g
Pencil: 7 g
Apple: 100 g
Chocolate bar: 50 g
```

**When to use grams:**
- Measuring ingredients in cooking
- Weighing small objects
- Medical doses (like medicine)
- Nutrition facts on food labels

**Example problem:**
```
Sarah buys 250 grams of flour for baking.
She uses 100 grams for a cake.
How much flour is left?

Answer: 250 - 100 = 150 grams
```

### Kilograms (kg)

**What is one kilogram?**
A kilogram is 1000 grams. Imagine a large textbook - it weighs about 1 kg.

**Relationship to grams:**
```
1 kg = 1000 g

This means:
3 kg = 3 √ó 1000 = 3000 g
5000 g = 5000 √∑ 1000 = 5 kg
```

**Common items and their masses:**
```
Newborn baby: 3-4 kg
10-year-old child: 30-35 kg
Adult person: 50-100 kg
Large dog: 20-30 kg
Bag of sugar: 1 kg
Liter of water: 1 kg
```

**When to use kilograms:**
- Weighing people
- Weighing bags of groceries
- Weighing larger objects

**Example problem:**
```
Daniel weighs 35 kilograms.
His backpack weighs 5 kilograms.
What is the total mass?

Answer: 35 + 5 = 40 kilograms
```

### Converting Between Grams and Kilograms

#### From Kilograms to Grams (Multiply by 1000)

**Example 1:**
```
Convert 2 kg to grams:
2 kg = 2 √ó 1000 = 2000 g
```

**Example 2:**
```
Convert 3.5 kg to grams:
3.5 kg = 3.5 √ó 1000 = 3500 g
```

**Why multiply?**
When you go from a bigger unit (kg) to a smaller unit (g), there are more of them!

#### From Grams to Kilograms (Divide by 1000)

**Example 1:**
```
Convert 4000 grams to kilograms:
4000 g = 4000 √∑ 1000 = 4 kg
```

**Example 2:**
```
Convert 7500 grams to kilograms:
7500 g = 7500 √∑ 1000 = 7.5 kg
```

**Why divide?**
When you go from a smaller unit (g) to a bigger unit (kg), there are fewer of them!

## Understanding Capacity

### What Does Capacity Mean?

**Capacity** is how much liquid something can hold. It's the volume inside a container.

**Examples:**
- A bottle holds 1 liter of water
- A cup holds 250 milliliters of juice
- A swimming pool holds 50,000 liters of water

### Milliliters (mL)

**What is one milliliter?**
A milliliter is a very small amount. It's about 20 drops of water.

**Relationship to teaspoons/tablespoons:**
```
1 teaspoon ‚âà 5 mL
1 tablespoon ‚âà 15 mL
```

**Common items and their capacity:**
```

Eyedropper: 1 mL
Teaspoon: 5 mL
Tablespoon: 15 mL
Cup: 250 mL
Small glass: 200 mL
Large bottle: 500 mL
```

**When to use milliliters:**
- Measuring medicine
- Small cooking ingredients
- Perfume or small bottles
- Nutrition labels (portion sizes)

**Example problem:**
```
A bottle of juice contains 500 milliliters.
You drink 150 milliliters.
How much is left?

Answer: 500 - 150 = 350 milliliters
```

### Liters (L)

**What is one liter?**
A liter is 1000 milliliters. It's about the size of a large milk carton.

**Relationship to milliliters:**
```
1 L = 1000 mL

This means:
2 L = 2 √ó 1000 = 2000 mL
3500 mL = 3500 √∑ 1000 = 3.5 L
```

**Common items and their capacity:**
```
Water bottle: 1-2 L
Milk carton: 1 L
Juice bottle: 1-2 L
Large pot: 5 L
Bathtub: 200-300 L
Swimming pool: 20,000-100,000 L
```

**When to use liters:**
- Water bottles
- Milk containers
- Fuel tanks
- Large quantities of liquid

**Example problem:**
```
A recipe needs 2 liters of milk.
How many milliliters is that?

Answer: 2 √ó 1000 = 2000 milliliters
```

### Converting Between Milliliters and Liters

#### From Liters to Milliliters (Multiply by 1000)

**Example 1:**

```

**Example 2:**
```
Convert 0.5 L to milliliters:

```

#### From Milliliters to Liters (Divide by 1000)

**Example 1:**
```
Convert 2500 milliliters to liters:
2500 mL = 2500 √∑ 1000 = 2.5 L
```

**Example 2:**
```
Convert 750 milliliters to liters:
750 mL = 750 √∑ 1000 = 0.75 L

**Example: Order from smallest to largest**

```

- 1.5 L = 1500 mL
- 500 mL = 500 mL
- 2000 mL = 2000 mL
- 0.5 L = 500 mL

Step 2: Order them
500 mL = 500 mL < 1500 mL < 2000 mL

Answer: 500 mL, 0.5 L, 1.5 L, 2000 mL
        (or 0.5 L, 0.5 L, 1.5 L, 2 L)
```

## Important Remember

### Mass vs Weight
- **Mass** stays the same everywhere (even in space!)
- **Weight** changes based on gravity
- In everyday use, we often use "weight" to mean "mass"
- In school, we use "mass" to be scientifically accurate

### The Special Metric Relationship
```
1 kilogram of pure water = 1 liter

This is true because the metric system
was designed around water!
```

## Summary

- **Mass** (grams and kilograms) measures how heavy something is
- **Capacity** (milliliters and liters) measures how much a container holds
- 1 kg = 1000 g
- 1 L = 1000 mL
- Convert by multiplying (bigger to smaller) or dividing (smaller to bigger)
