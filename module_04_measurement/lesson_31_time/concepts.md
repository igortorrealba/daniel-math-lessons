# Lesson 31: Time

## Core Concepts

### What is Time?
Time is how we measure and track moments, events, and durations. We use clocks and calendars to keep track of time.

## Units of Time

### Seconds (s)
- The smallest unit we usually measure
- About the time it takes to blink
- Used for: Sports timings, cooking, quick events

### Minutes (min)
- 60 seconds = 1 minute
- About the time to drink a glass of water
- Used for: Daily activities, class periods, appointments

### Hours (h)
- 60 minutes = 1 hour
- About the time for a short class or movie
- Used for: Work shifts, travel times, sleep duration

### Days
- 24 hours = 1 day
- From midnight to midnight
- Used for: Planning activities, counting time

### Weeks
- 7 days = 1 week
- Monday through Sunday
- Used for: School schedules, work weeks

### Months
- About 28-31 days = 1 month
- Different months have different lengths
- Used for: Planning, appointments, dates

### Years
- 12 months = 1 year
- 365 days = 1 year (366 in leap years)
- Used for: Age, historical dates, long-term planning

## Time Conversion Chart

```
60 seconds = 1 minute
60 minutes = 1 hour
24 hours = 1 day
7 days = 1 week
About 4 weeks = 1 month
12 months = 1 year
```

## Types of Clocks

### Analog Clocks
- Have moving hands (hour, minute, sometimes second)
- Three hands usually: hour (short), minute (long), second (thin)
- Face shows 12 hours
- You read the position of the hands

### Digital Clocks
- Show time as numbers
- Format: HH:MM or HH:MM:SS
- Shows 24-hour or 12-hour time
- Example: 14:30 or 2:30 PM

## Key Terms

- **Analog clock**: A clock with moving hands
- **Digital clock**: A clock showing numbers
- **Elapsed time**: How much time has passed
- **A.M.**: Before noon (midnight to 11:59 AM)
- **P.M.**: After noon (12:00 PM to 11:59 PM)
- **Midnight**: 12:00 AM (start of the day)
- **Noon**: 12:00 PM (middle of the day)
