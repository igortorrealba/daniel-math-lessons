## Understanding Fractions and Decimals

### Story Time! ðŸ•
Imagine you have a pizza cut into 10 equal slices. If you eat 3 slices, you've eaten:
- **Fraction way**: 3/10 of the pizza
- **Decimal way**: 0.3 of the pizza

Both mean the SAME thing! They're just different ways to write it.

### Converting Fractions with Denominator 10 to Decimals

**Rule**: The numerator becomes the decimal place!

#### Example 1: 4/10
```
4/10 = 0.4

Why? Because we have 4 out of 10 parts
     = 4 tenths
     = 0.4
```

#### Example 2: 7/10
```
7/10 = 0.7

We have 7 out of 10 parts
     = 7 tenths
     = 0.7
```

#### Example 3: 10/10
```
10/10 = 1.0

We have all 10 parts
     = 10 tenths
     = one whole
     = 1.0
```

### Converting Fractions with Denominator 100 to Decimals

**Rule**: The numerator becomes the last two decimal places!

#### Example 1: 25/100
```
25/100 = 0.25

Why? Because we have 25 out of 100 parts
     = 25 hundredths
     = 0.25 (the 2 is in the tenths place, the 5 is in the hundredths place)
```

#### Example 2: 7/100
```
7/100 = 0.07

We have 7 out of 100 parts
     = 7 hundredths
     = 0.07 (notice the zero! it shows we have no tenths)
```

#### Example 3: 50/100
```
50/100 = 0.50

We have 50 out of 100 parts
     = 50 hundredths
     = 0.50 (same as 0.5)
```

## Real-World Applications ðŸ’°

### Money Example
```
If you have $3.25:
- Decimal way: 3 dollars and 25 cents
- Fraction way: 3 and 25/100 dollars

The decimal makes it SO easy to count money!
```

### Measurement Example
```
If something measures 1.5 meters:
- Decimal way: 1 and 5/10 meters
- Fraction way: 1 and 5/10 meters

Both tell us it's 1 meter and half of another meter.
```

## Summary
- Fractions and decimals show the SAME value, just written differently
- With denominator 10: fraction numerator = tenths decimal place
- With denominator 100: fraction numerator = hundredths decimal places
- Decimals make comparisons and real-world applications easier!
