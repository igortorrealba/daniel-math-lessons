# Lesson 28: Practice Exercises

## Section 1: Money Addition Problems

1. Lucas has $4.50. His mom gives him $3.25. How much money does he have now?

2. A notebook costs $2.75. A pen costs $1.50. What is the total cost?

3. Sarah found $5.00 in coins. Later she found $2.75 more. How much did she find altogether?

4. Daniel's lunch cost $6.50 at home. His snack cost $2.25. What was the total cost?

5. A toy car costs $8.99. A toy train costs $6.50. How much do both toys cost together?

## Section 2: Money Subtraction and Change Problems

6. Emma has $10.00. She buys a book for $5.75. How much change does she get?

7. Your dad gives you $20.00. You spend $13.45. How much money is left?

8. A game costs $15.99. You have $25.00. How much will you have left after buying it?

9. Tom has $50.00. He buys groceries for $38.60. How much does he have left?

10. You have $7.50. A magazine costs $3.25. How much change will you get?

## Section 3: Measurement Problems

11. A piece of string is 3.5 meters long. Another piece is 2.3 meters long. What is the total length?

12. A rope measures 8.7 meters. You cut off 3.2 meters. How much rope is left?

13. The water bottle holds 1.25 liters. The pitcher holds 2.75 liters. How much more does the pitcher hold?

14. Daniel's height is 1.45 meters. His sister is 1.62 meters tall. How much taller is his sister?

15. A plank is 4.8 meters long. A board is 2.3 meters long. How much longer is the plank?

## Section 4: Multi-Step Problems

16. Maria buys a sandwich for $7.50 and juice for $2.25. She pays with $20.00. How much change does she get?

17. Tom has $50.00. He buys a book ($12.50) and a pen ($4.75). How much money does he have left?

18. At the store, you find:
    - Notebooks: $3.50 each
    - Pens: $1.75 each
    You buy 1 notebook and 1 pen. If you pay with $10.00, what's your change?

19. Sarah measures two ribbons:
    - Red ribbon: 2.3 meters
    - Blue ribbon: 1.8 meters
    She joins them together but then cuts off 0.5 meters. How long is the final ribbon?

20. A bucket holds 5.5 liters. It already has 2.3 liters of water. You add 1.5 liters more. How much space is left in the bucket?

## Section 5: Estimation Problems

**For each problem, estimate first, then solve exactly:**

21. Estimate, then calculate: 4.8 + 3.2 = ?

22. Estimate, then calculate: 9.7 - 5.1 = ?

23. Estimate, then calculate: 7.45 + 2.60 = ?

24. Estimate, then calculate: 15.5 - 8.3 = ?

25. Estimate, then calculate: 3.25 + 4.75 + 2.50 = ?

## Section 6: Real-World Applications

26. At the bakery:
    - Bread costs $4.50
    - Milk costs $3.25
    - Cheese costs $5.75
    How much is the total? If you pay with $20.00, how much change?

27. Three friends share costs:
    - Movie ticket: $12.50
    - Popcorn: $6.75
    - Drink: $4.50
    What's the total? If they split equally between 3 people, how much does each pay?

28. A jogger ran:
    - Monday: 3.5 km
    - Wednesday: 4.2 km
    - Friday: 3.8 km
    How far did they run total?

29. A recipe needs 2.5 cups of flour, 1.75 cups of sugar, and 0.5 cups of butter. How much total ingredients?

30. Emma has $25.00. She wants to buy:
    - Notebook: $4.50
    - Pen set: $8.75
    - Book: $9.99
    Does she have enough? If yes, how much change? If no, how much more does she need?

## Challenge Problems

**For advanced learners:**

31. At a restaurant, the meal costs $28.50. Tax is $2.28. You want to leave a tip of about $6.00. How much total should you pay?

32. Your allowance is $15.00 per week. You spend an average of $8.75 per week. After 4 weeks, how much have you saved?

33. A store is having a sale. A shirt costs $24.99 but is discounted. After the discount, it costs $18.75. How much did you save?

34. Three books cost: $12.50, $8.99, and $15.75. You have $40.00. How much change do you get?

35. A water tank starts with 50.5 liters. You remove 12.3 liters, then add 8.7 liters. How much water is in the tank now?
