#### Simple Addition
**Problem:** Maria has $3.50. Her friend gives her $2.25. How much money does Maria have now?

```
Step 1: Identify what we know
   - Maria starts with $3.50
   - She gets $2.25 more
   - We need to find the total

Step 2: Choose operation ‚Üí ADDITION

Step 3: Estimate first
   - $3.50 is about $4
   - $2.25 is about $2
   - $4 + $2 = $6 (estimate)

Step 4: Set up and solve
     $3.50
   + $2.25
   -------
     $5.75

Step 5: Check
   - Our estimate was $6
   - Our answer $5.75 is close to $6 ‚úì
   - Makes sense!
```

**Answer: Maria has $5.75**

#### Subtraction and Change
**Problem:** Daniel has $10.00. He buys a book that costs $7.45. How much change does he get?

```
Step 1: Identify what we know

Step 3: Estimate first

Step 4: Set up and solve
     $10.00

Step 5: Check
   - Our estimate was $3
   - Our answer $2.55 is close to $3 ‚úì
   - Makes sense!
```

**Answer: Daniel gets $2.55 in change**

## Multi-Step Problems

**Problem:** Emma buys two items:
- A pencil costs $1.25
- An eraser costs $0.75

She pays with a $5.00 bill. How much change does she get?

```
Step 1: Understand the problem
   - Need to find total spent
   - Then find the change

Step 2: FIRST - Add the costs
     $1.25
   + $0.75
   -------
     $2.00 (total spent)

Step 3: THEN - Subtract from payment
     $5.00
   - $2.00
   -------
     $3.00 (change)

Step 4: Estimate to check
   - $1.25 + $0.75 ‚âà $1 + $1 = $2 ‚úì
   - $5 - $2 = $3 ‚úì
   - Our answer matches the estimate!
```

**Answer: Emma gets $3.00 in change**

## Estimation Strategies

### Strategy 1: Rounding to Whole Numbers

**Use when:** You want a quick, rough answer

```
Example Problem:
Tom has $8.60 and buys something for $3.45.
About how much will he have left?

Round to whole numbers:
   $8.60 ‚Üí $9
   $3.45 ‚Üí $3
   $9 - $3 = $6 (estimate)

Exact answer:
   $8.60 - $3.45 = $5.15

The estimate tells us the answer is close to $6,
and the exact answer $5.15 makes sense!
```

### Strategy 2: Rounding to Tenths

**Use when:** You want a more precise estimate

```
Example Problem:
A container holds 2.47 liters.
Another holds 1.35 liters.
About how much total?

Round to tenths:
   2.47 ‚Üí 2.5
   1.35 ‚Üí 1.4
   2.5 + 1.4 = 3.9 (estimate)

Exact answer:
   2.47 + 1.35 = 3.82

The estimate 3.9 is very close to 3.82!
```

### Strategy 3: Compensating

**Use when:** One number is very close to a round number

```
Example Problem:
7.98 + 3.25 = ?

Notice: 7.98 is almost 8!

Think: If I round 7.98 to 8:
   8 + 3.25 = 11.25

But I added 0.02 too much, so:
   11.25 - 0.02 = 11.23

Exact answer:
   7.98 + 3.25 = 11.23 ‚úì

(Much easier than calculating with 7.98!)
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Not Lining Up Decimal Points
```
Wrong:  $5.25           Right:  $5.25
      + $2.1                  + $2.10
      -------                 -------
      $5.46 ‚ùå               $7.35 ‚úì
```

### Mistake 2: Forgetting to Check Reasonableness
```
Problem: $10.00 - $3.50 = ?

If you get $6.50, stop and ask:
"Does $6.50 make sense?"
Yes! It's less than $10 but more than $3. ‚úì

If you got $13.50, stop and ask:
"Does $13.50 make sense?"
No! I started with only $10. ‚ùå
```

### Mistake 3: Not Reading the Question Carefully
```
Problem: "Maria has $5.00. She buys a book
for $2.50 and a pen for $1.75.
How much change does she have?"

Wrong thinking: 5.00 + 2.50 + 1.75 = ?
Right thinking: 5.00 - (2.50 + 1.75) = ?
               or: (5.00 - 2.50) - 1.75 = ?
```
